Those who have had the pleasure of ever engaging with my father, will attest to the fact that his wealth of knowledge coupled with his compassion is unparalleled in this universe. Love you Papa.

Vatsal, 2018

My father became my father 25 years ago. It is fair to say that he’s a bit of an expert at the job. I’m told by people that it isn’t easy to be a father. And I believe them because fathers are truly super-heroes in every day clothing. At least, mine is. My papa gave me my name. It’s the name of the river that ran outside his childhood home. My father carried me on his shoulders and put up with my silly tantrums when I was a toddler. My papa would tell me stories over dinner when I refused to eat. And then when I did start to take an interest in food…he was the one who taught me how to make my first dish. An omelette! And I still make omelettes the same way.
As I grew he helped me make important life decisions and guided me with his words and wisdom. He along with my mother became my rock and someone I relied on to guide me the right way in life.
As the years have gone by, I feel I understand my father more and more. I am able to have a one minute conversation in the morning about moving cars in the drive-way to hour long conversations about ethics and society with him. He challenges me to be the best version of myself and leads by example.
I share his passion for writing and his thoughts and opinions are often echoed by me. I truly am his daughter and what a privilege it is.
As he turns 50… I only wish him success, peace and many happy memories for the future.

Vitasta, 2018

''Walk, not a step behind and not a step ahead, walk with me'', were your first words when we started walking together in 1992. What a walk it has been that has transcended time and imagination in every way that counts. A walk on a path that only a few dare to dream with your vision so clear that it seemed to me that you had a crystal ball. This walk seemed even more beautiful as Vitasta, Vatsal, Vyas and then little Snowy joined us and gleefully skipped along with us. You travelled the world and came back with stories expertly spinning them into tales of wonder as we listened with rapt attention. Your generosity lay in sharing those experiences with us and then leading the way to those places creating memories so special and magical. You saw humanity at its best and its worst on your walks around the world. And then you wrote stories to share with the rest of the world.
On your birthday, I pray that you continue to see the magic in everything that crosses your path and that I remain your companion on all the paths you are yet to walk.

Anita, 2018
